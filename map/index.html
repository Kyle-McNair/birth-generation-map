What are all of these dots?

  • All of the dots represents a rough distribution of population density by birth generation.
    • Population was aggregated through the U.S. Census Block Groups.
    • Dots were only distributed in Block Groups that had Blocks with a population above zero.
    • Dots are categorized and colored by birth generation
  • Each dot was randomly placed within each U.S. Census Block Group
    • Each dot does not actually portray the specific location of where people live. It only suggests the density of the birth generation inside the Block Group.
    • For insance, one Generation Z dot at the scale of 1 dot = 25 people means 25 people who are in the Generation Z group live in that area (Block Group). It does not mean 25 Generation Z people live in that one location that the dot is placed on.
    • The scale of the dot placement does not represent true total population of the whole area.

About the Project

Developing this project got me inspired by several visualizations including, but not limited to:

  • Robert Manduca's Where Are The Jobs?
  • Kyle Walker's Educational Attainment in America

Data Sources:

Scrollytelling Data:

  • GIS & Tabular data at the United States level, State Level, Urban Area level, and U.S. Census Block Group level were all provided by the National Historical GIS (NHGIS) 2014-2018 American Community Survey, and 2009-2013 American Community Survey (ACS).
  • Original age classificaiton of the birth generations were from PEW Research.

Generation Dot Map:

  • Dots were randomly generated from each Census Block Group based on the NGHIS 2014-2018 ACS & NHGIS 2009-2013 ACS.
  • Basemap and vector tilesets hosting by Mapbox.
